Case:
Patient Information
- Age: 31 years
- Sex: Male

Presenting Complaint
- Progressive swelling of the right knee
- Intermittent pain involving the same knee

History of Present Illness
The patient reports a long-standing problem involving his right knee that began several years ago. Symptoms started with gradual swelling and intermittent pain, which tended to worsen after physical or strenuous activities. Over time, the swelling became more pronounced and the pain episodes increased in frequency.

He also reports a recent reduction in appetite and unintentional weight loss over a short period. He denies respiratory symptoms and does not recall experiencing night sweats or chills.

There is a history of trauma to the affected leg following a motorcycle accident prior to the onset of symptoms.

Past Medical and Treatment History
- Previously evaluated at another healthcare facility and advised to undergo further diagnostic evaluation
- Chose alternative treatments, including massage and herbal remedies, over several years
- Used analgesics and oral antibiotics intermittently
- No known drug or food allergies

Family and Social History
- No known family history of malignancy or genetic disease
- No known contact with individuals diagnosed with tuberculosis

Clinical Course
Despite prolonged alternative treatment approaches, the patient noted no improvement. Instead, the condition of the right knee progressively worsened over several years, prompting reevaluation and further medical attention.

Initial Clinical Impression
- Chronic, progressive knee disorder of unclear etiology
- Differential considerations include post-traumatic, inflammatory, infectious, or other chronic joint conditions

Tag: tuberculosis, headache, hearing loss, lymph node, nasal obstruction, nasopharyngeal disease, nasopharyngeal neoplasm, nasopharynx, treatment
Case:

Patient Information
- Age: 17 years
- Sex: Female
- Family History: No known family history of tuberculosis

Presenting Complaint
- Bilateral neck swellings
- Nasal obstruction

History of Present Illness
The patient presented with **multiple swellings on both sides of the neck**, noted over an unspecified period. These were associated with **nasal obstruction**. No additional symptoms were described.

Physical Examination
- Numerous **bilateral cervical lymph node enlargements** were noted on examination

Endoscopic Findings
- Nasal endoscopy revealed a **polypoid mass** arising from the **superior and posterior walls of the nasopharynx**

Initial Imaging
- Imaging of the head and neck demonstrated a **nasopharyngeal mass** with a central area of reduced density, suggestive of internal tissue changes
- Multiple cervical lymph node enlargements were also observed

Initial Diagnostic Step
- A tissue biopsy of the lesion was performed

Initial Clinical Impression
- Nasopharyngeal mass with associated bilateral cervical lymphadenopathy of unclear etiology
- Definitive diagnosis pending histopathological evaluation
